So what's with all these properties that sound pretty similar? Isn't a margin like a border to begin with?

Well, sort of. There are actually three similar properties that affect all of your HTML elements - padding, border, and margin. They all extend away from your element in that order, and in all four directions to help space things out on your page. Or not space them, if that's what you're going for. You may not actually see them, but they are there.

If you've ever had a picture mounted in a frame and then hung on your wall, it's actually kind of similar. To start, think of your HTML content (text, picture, link, etc.) as the picture in the frame. If your frame has a mat to it, where the overall frame is bigger than the picture, maybe a white area to help center the image, that's like HTML padding. The actual metal or wood frame is your HTML border. You don't see the padding, but it provides any extra space between your content and the border.

If that's padding and border, there's nothing left in the picture frame analogy, so what's a margin? Well, let's say you're hanging two pictures on your wall... there's probably a bit of a gap between them, unless you hang them so close that they touch. That gap in between is your HTML margin. Similar to padding, you don't really see it, but it helps space things between your other elements.
